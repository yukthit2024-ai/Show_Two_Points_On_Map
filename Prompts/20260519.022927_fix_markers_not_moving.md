# Implementation Plan - Fixing Marker Real-Time Movement Visibility

This plan addresses why the markers appeared static and implements concrete fixes to make real-time movement perfectly visible and robust.

## Why the Points Appeared "Not Moving"
1. **Microscopic Step Size**: 
   At a standard zoom level like `14.5` (neighborhood view), the scale is roughly `6.7` meters per pixel. A movement of **1 meter** translates to only **0.15 pixels** on the screen. It would take ~7 seconds of movement for the marker to shift by a single pixel, making them look completely static and unmoving to the human eye.
2. **Missing View Invalidation**:
   Depending on the Android device's hardware acceleration state, MapLibre's internal redrawing loop might not trigger instantly on a coordinate change if there is no user interaction.

---

## Technical Solution

### 1. Increase Step Size to 15 Meters
We will increase the movement step size from 1 meter (`0.001` km) to **15 meters** (`0.015` km) per second. At zoom `14.5`, 15 meters represents a clear, smooth, and easily noticeable shift of **~2.2 pixels** every second, making the real-time random walk animation perfectly visible and engaging!

### 2. Force Map Redraw
After updating the marker coordinates, we will call `mapView.invalidate()` to force the map view to immediately redraw its contents. This guarantees that coordinate updates are rendered on screen instantly.

### 3. Add Diagnostic Logcat Logging
We will insert standard Android Logcat logging (`android.util.Log.d`) inside the loop, printing the coordinates at every 1-second tick so that the execution can be easily monitored and verified in real time.
